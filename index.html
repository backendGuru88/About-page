Books

Health economics in Dentistry by R. Tuominen

Health Economics in Dentistry, 2nd edition by Risto Tuominen, provides a detailed look at economic principles applied to dental care. It covers demand, supply, costs, government regulation, payment mechanisms, and patient access. The book explains cost-effectiveness analysis (CEA) and health technology assessment (HTA) for evaluating new dental treatments. It also explores the economic value of preventive dentistry and the role of dental insurance in financing care. Concluding with trends in digital and personalized dentistry, the book is a key resource for dental professionals, helping them make informed decisions on resource allocation and technology adoption (Tuominen, 2018).

Healthcare Management by Kieran Walshe and Judith Smith third Edition.

The third edition of Healthcare Management provides essential guidance on managing healthcare organizations, focusing on strategic planning, operational efficiency, leadership, policy, ethics, and financial management. It emphasizes setting clear goals, optimizing processes, fostering effective leadership, ensuring ethical practices, and maintaining financial sustainability—all crucial for navigating the complexities of healthcare management (Robinson, 2006).

Epidemiology: An Introduction (Rothman et al., 2024)

This foundational epidemiology text explains the principles of health-related events in populations, covering causation, study design, disease frequency, association measures, bias, and data analysis. Key topics include the Bradford Hill criteria for causality, strengths and limitations of various study designs, and statistical methods for analyzing real-world data. The book also emphasizes practical applications in monitoring and evaluating interventions, making it an accessible and valuable introduction to epidemiology.

Essentials of biostatistics for public health (Sullivan, 2022)

Essentials of Biostatistics for Public Health introduces core biostatistical concepts, covering probability, data collection, descriptive and inferential statistics, correlation, regression, survival analysis, and epidemiological methods. With clear explanations and practical examples, it’s an essential guide for applying biostatistics in public health research, featuring tools like R and SPSS.
